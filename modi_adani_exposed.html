01 · The Core Loop

THE ARRANGEMENT, EXPLAINED SIMPLY

Since 2014, a documented pattern has emerged: government contracts, privatised public assets, and diplomatic cover go to Adani Group — which had ~$7 billion in net worth in 2014 and rocketed past $100 billion by 2022. In return, Adani Group is BJP's biggest documented corporate backer — buying electoral bonds, funding aligned media, and supporting the ruling political ecosystem. Meanwhile, taxes on common goods went up, student exam systems collapsed, and public infrastructure is now owned by a private monopoly with zero prior experience.

This is not conspiracy. These are documented, reported, court-verified facts. Sources listed at the bottom.

02 · Timeline of Documented Events
2014
Modi Wins. Adani's Private Jet Flies Him to Delhi for Oath Ceremony.
Gautam Adani's private plane flew PM Modi from Ahmedabad to Delhi for the swearing-in. Both are from Gujarat. Adani net worth at the time: ~$7 billion.
2016
Demonetisation — 99.3% of Notes Returned. Nothing Changed.
₹500 & ₹1000 notes scrapped overnight. RBI's own report confirmed 99.3% of all demonetised notes came back — meaning black money wasn't destroyed. Small businesses and farmers devastated. Large corporates who were already digital? Unaffected.
2019
6 Major Airports Handed to Adani — a Company With ZERO Airport Experience.
Airports Authority of India privatised Ahmedabad, Lucknow, Mangalore, Jaipur, Thiruvananthapuram, and Guwahati airports. Adani Group, which had never operated an airport, won all 6. Mumbai airport (CSIA) followed via GVK acquisition.
2020
PM CARES Fund Created — Exempt from RTI and CAG Audit.
March 28, 2020. A COVID relief fund created by PM Modi's office. Not subject to the Right to Information Act. Not audited by the Comptroller and Auditor General of India. Collected ₹10,990 crore+ per government figures, but independent verification is legally impossible. Adani Group was among the donors.
2022
Adani Becomes Asia's Richest Person. Briefly World's 2nd Richest.
From $7B in 2014 to a peak of ~$150 billion in 2022. The fastest wealth accumulation in India's corporate history — coinciding precisely with 8 years of BJP government contracts and privatisation.
2023 JAN
Hindenburg Research Report Drops. Adani Stocks Lose $100B+.
US short-seller Hindenburg Research released a 100+ page report alleging stock manipulation, accounting fraud, and money laundering through a web of offshore shell companies in Mauritius and the Cayman Islands. Adani stocks crashed. BJP government's response: silence, then deflection. Opposition MPs who tried to raise it in Parliament were drowned out.
2023 AUG
Hindenburg's Follow-Up: SEBI Chief Madhabi Puri Buch Implicated.
Hindenburg alleged that SEBI chairperson Madhabi Puri Buch and her husband had investments in the same offshore funds used in the alleged Adani stock scheme. She was the regulator investigating Adani. She denied the allegations. SEBI's investigation into Adani went quiet.
2023
Adani Acquires NDTV. Independent Voice Silenced.
Adani Group took over NDTV — one of India's most respected news channels — forcing out founders Prannoy Roy and Radhika Roy. Editorial independence deteriorated. India's press freedom ranking: 159th out of 180 countries (RSF Press Freedom Index 2023).
2024 FEB
Supreme Court Strikes Down Electoral Bonds as Unconstitutional.
Electoral bonds — anonymous political funding instruments — were declared unconstitutional. Data revealed BJP received ~55% of all bonds (₹6,566 crore). Adani-linked companies were among purchasers. Multiple companies under ED/CBI investigation had bought bonds — and cases slowed or stopped afterward.
2024 JUN
NEET Paper Leak — 24 Lakh Students' Futures Destroyed.
NEET-UG 2024 exam paper was leaked across multiple states before the exam. 24+ lakh students affected. UGC-NET was cancelled just one day after being conducted. CBI launched investigation. NTA (National Testing Agency), under the government, faced massive accountability questions. Students who spent years preparing lost their chance.
2024 NOV
US Department of Justice INDICTS Gautam Adani for Bribery.
The US DOJ indicted Gautam Adani for allegedly paying ~$265 million (₹2,200+ crore) in bribes to Indian state government officials to secure solar energy contracts for Adani Green Energy. The SEC also filed charges. Kenya and other countries cancelled Adani contracts. Modi government's reaction: protective silence.

06 · PM CARES: The Untouchable Fund
⚠ RTI EXEMPT · CAG EXEMPT · AUDIT EXEMPT

PM CARES FUND

Created on March 28, 2020 — just days into the COVID lockdown — PM CARES (Prime Minister's Citizen Assistance and Relief in Emergency Situations) is a fund chaired by the PM himself. It is not subject to the Right to Information Act. It is not audited by the Comptroller and Auditor General — India's constitutional auditor. The government argued in courts it is a "public charitable trust," not a government fund — yet its chairman is the Prime Minister of India and it uses government infrastructure. The existing PM National Relief Fund was already functional. A parallel, unaccountable fund was created. No Indian citizen can legally know who donated, how much, or where the money went.

Adani Group donated to PM CARES Fund. The exact amount is unknown because RTI requests about individual donors are rejected. This is legal in PM CARES. It is not legal for the PM National Relief Fund. The difference? The old fund has Parliament oversight.

07 · The Regulator Problem

WHEN THE WATCHDOG WORKS FOR THE WOLF

India's financial regulator SEBI was tasked with investigating the Hindenburg allegations against Adani. But Hindenburg's August 2023 follow-up report alleged that SEBI chairperson Madhabi Puri Buch herself — and her husband — held investments in the same obscure Mauritius-based offshore funds that were allegedly used by the Adani Group in the alleged stock manipulation scheme.

She denied the allegations. The government stood by her. SEBI's investigation produced minimal findings. The Supreme Court's committee on the matter noted that SEBI had investigated only 11 of 24 allegations in the Hindenburg report and asked SEBI to expedite — but no major action followed.

When the person investigating you profits from the same system you're accused of manipulating, justice doesn't arrive.
